Comuna 13 + The Narcos Trail

Monday · June 29
Late morning / afternoon

🎨 Comuna 13 — graffiti, street food, cable car

The most worthwhile thing in the city and zero museum energy. A barrio that went from world's-most-dangerous to open-air street-art gallery — local guides, rap, breakdance, the famous outdoor escalators. ~3 hrs.

Food's built in: church empanadas, patacón con hogao, and mango ice cream with salt + lime. Most tours add a metrocable ride for the views. Book it.

Tonight · kick off the trail

🎬 The Narcos (Netflix) Trail

Start tonight on the real-life sites behind the show — a guided history tour (local guides give the actual context, not the glorified version). The iconic stops:

• Barrio Pablo Escobar (El Dorado) — the estate he built, murals of his face still up
• Parque Memorial Inflexión — the old Mónaco building site, now a victims' memorial
• La Catedral — the mountain-top "prison" he built himself, panoramic city views
• Jardines Montesacro — his grave
• Los Olivos rooftop — where it all ended in '93

Book a guided Pablo Escobar / Narcos sites tour. La Catedral is up the mountain, so if the group wants the full version, roll the rest into tomorrow morning before Guatapé.

Guatapé Day Trip

Tuesday · June 30
All day · out of the city

🪨 El Peñol Rock + the painted town

~2 hrs each way. Climb the 740 steps up the giant monolith for a 360° view over a maze of lake islands, then roam Guatapé's insanely colorful streets. Late June = dry season, good odds of clear skies.

Tours leave ~8 AM, back ~7 PM, usually with a boat ride + lunch (~$20–35 each). Eat trucha (lake trout) by the water. Tour options.

Fruit · Street Food · Send-off

Wednesday · July 1
Morning · do this hungry

🍈 Exotic Fruits Tour @ Plaza Minorista

2.5 hrs tasting 13–15 Colombian fruits you literally can't get back home, ending with a fresh juice blended in front of you. Small group, real working market, zero tourist trap. ~$24 each.

Book ahead for the crew: Real City Tours or GetYourGuide. Best food thing we'll do all trip.

FRUIT CHEAT SHEET

Colombia grows fruit that never leaves the country. Hunt these down — pocket change at any jugo stand. Rule for the trip: order one you can't pronounce, every single day.

🟢LuloCitrus-kiwi tang. Best as a "lulada" juice.
🤍GuanábanaSoursop — creamy, custardy, juice it with milk.
👑MangostinoMangosteen. Sweet white segments. The queen.
🟠GranadillaCrack it, slurp the sweet seedy gel.
💜MaracuyáPassionfruit — sour, electric, top-tier juice.
🎃ZapoteDense orange flesh, almost pumpkin-sweet.
🟤BorojóFunky, earthy — locals swear it's an aphrodisiac.
🟡MamoncilloStreet snack — pop it, suck the tangy pulp.
🟠UchuvaGoldenberry — sweet-tart little pop.
🐉Pitaya amarillaYellow dragonfruit. Sweet... and a known laxative.
🥥ChontaduroSavory peach-palm + salt. The true oddity.
🥭Mango bicheGreen mango, salt + lime. The street staple.

Know Before You Go

Book ahead
Lock the Comuna 13, Narcos, fruit and Guatapé tours before you land — they fill up. One person books for the group.
Cash
Carry pesos for markets, carts & juice stands. Cards fine for restaurants/rooftops.
Narcos tour
Pick a guide that tells the victims' side, not the glorified one — it lands better and it's the respectful way to see it.
Safety
"No dar papaya" — don't flash phones/jewelry on the street. Stick to Poblado, Laureles, Provenza, Envigado at night. Uber, not street taxis, after dark.
Weather
Late June = dry-ish, 70s–80s°F, eternal spring. Pack a light layer for Guatapé.
Pacing
Big lunches, lighter dinners, hydrate around the aguardiente. Save the real send-off for Wednesday.
